Moving to the West

In the late 19th century, Buddhism began to make its way to America, where it was initially met with a mixture of fascination and misinterpretation. Asian immigrant communities established the first Buddhist temples on American soil, but it was not until the mid-20th century that Buddhism began to gain wider popularity among Americans, influenced in part by the Beat Generation and the counterculture movement of the 1960s. As Buddhism has continued to grow in popularity in the United States, it has also faced challenges, including instances of cultural appropriation and misunderstanding, as well as a process of adaptation and transformation as it finds its place in the American cultural landscape.
